<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="overview">Overview</h2>



<p>FISPACT-II is an enhanced multiphysics, inventory and source-term code system developed by the UK Atomic Energy Authority at Culham. It provides advanced predictive, spectral and temporal simulation methods for neutron and charged-particle interactions using comprehensive nuclear data.</p>



<p>FISPACT-II has applications in:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>magnetic and inertial confinement fusion</li>



<li>advanced fission Generation IV and beyond</li>



<li>other advanced energy and fuel systems</li>



<li>high energy and accelerator physics</li>



<li>medical applications, including isotope production</li>



<li>earth exploration and astrophysics</li>



<li>homeland security</li>



<li>materials science</li>



<li>nuclear maintenance and decommissioning</li>



<li>… and more</li>
</ul>



<p>FISPACT-II is capable of various analysis techniques including:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>activation analysis</li>



<li>transmutation calculations</li>



<li>depletion and burn-up simulations</li>



<li>decay modelling</li>



<li>source definition</li>



<li>full inventories</li>



<li>displacement per atom (dpa) calculations</li>



<li>Kerma calculations</li>



<li>Primary damage (PKA) spectra</li>



<li>gas / radionuclide production simulations</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="key-benefits">Key benefits</h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>nuclear data agnostic – you can pick the best nuclear data library for your neutron, proton or other calculations</li>



<li>Application Programming Interface (API) allows easy and safe linkage to other codes</li>



<li>validated – used on ITER, DEMO and STEP for fusion, and on fission power plants (e.g. characterising fuel rods, shut down dose calculations)</li>



<li>pathways analysis (where does key nuclei come from) can help with material engineering</li>



<li>can identify radioactive emission of a given sample</li>



<li>can predict decay heat and gamma dose</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="software">Software details</h2>



<p>FISPACT-II supports users in:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>full processing of ENDF-6 nuclear data with complete TENDL data and covariances</li>



<li>self-shielding factors calculation</li>



<li>temperature dependence modelling</li>



<li>thin / thick target yields</li>



<li>robust pathway analysis</li>



<li>Monte-Carlo sensitivity and uncertainty quantification</li>



<li>covariance data propagation</li>



<li>for a given neutron flux, activation/transmutation of fusion materials/metals/alloys can be calculated</li>



<li>radiation shielding calculations can be completed to help design new power plants</li>



<li>displacements per atom (DPA) can be calculated to quantify radiation damage in materials from nuclear collisions and reactions</li>



<li>shut down dose calculations for fission and fusion power plants to help design safe operations and shielding needs</li>



<li>waste calculations for the running of future fusion and fission power plants</li>



<li>medical radio-isotope sector – understanding design, target needs and safe operation</li>



<li>ability for FISPACT-II to model waste processing using irradiation</li>



<li>waste assessment for nuclear materials prior to decommissioning</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="technical-specifications">Technical specifications</h3>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="programming-languages">Programming languages</h4>



<p>C-LANGUAGE, C++, FORTRAN 2003, PYTHON</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="libraries-used">Libraries used</h4>



<p>TENDL-2017, ENDF/B-VIII.1, JEFF-4, JENDL-5.0, CENDL-3.2, GEFY-6.2, and more.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="processing-tools">Processing tools</h4>



<p>PREPRO, NJOY, CALENDF</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="system-requirements">System requirements</h4>



<p>The memory requirement at run-time is case dependent, dynamic allocation, usually not above 1 Gb of RAM.</p>



<p>64-bit Linux, macOS and Windows executables, built using GNU Fortran compilers on:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>macOS Ventura (Intel)</li>



<li>Ubuntu 22.04</li>



<li>Windows 10</li>
</ul>



<p>The bin subdirectory contains FISPACT-II executable compiled under different operating systems (Linux, macOS and Windows).</p>



<p>FISPACT-II has been tested at the NEA Data Bank on:</p>



<h5 class="wp-block-heading" id="windows-10">WINDOWS 10</h5>



<p>COMPUTER: Dell Precision with Intel(R) Core (TM) i7-7600U CPU at 2.80 GHz, RAM: 16.0 GB</p>



<h5 class="wp-block-heading" id="linux-ubuntu-20-04">LINUX – Ubuntu 20.04</h5>



<p>COMPUTER: Dell Precision M6800 with Intel(R) Core (TM) i7-4800MQ CPU at 2.70 GHz x 8, RAM: 16.0 GB</p>
